Affiliate marketing works when offers, traffic, tracking and partner expectations operate as one system.

A payout alone does not make an offer profitable. Sustainable affiliate growth depends on economics, data quality, operational discipline and relationships working together.

Many teams do not have a traffic problem. They have a coordination problem: commercial terms live in messages, tracking lives in a separate platform, campaign decisions are made from partial data, and the offer mix is not aligned with the actual audience.

My role is to connect those parts. I review the commercial model, campaign structure, data quality and operating process, then turn the findings into a prioritized plan that the team can implement.

02 /

Scope of work

01

Affiliate strategy and operating model

Clarify goals, traffic sources, commercial terms, reporting responsibilities and the decisions each stakeholder must own.

02

Campaign and funnel optimization

Analyze ROI, EPC, CVR, CPA and drop-off points to identify where performance is lost and which tests deserve budget.

03

Tracking and attribution

Review pixels, postbacks, click IDs, tracker-to-network communication and discrepancies between spend and revenue data.

04

Partner and network relations

Prepare traffic descriptions, conditions, caps, payout discussions and communication that supports durable commercial relationships.

05

Offer and inventory decisions

Compare offer economics, GEO fit, conversion flows and traffic requirements instead of choosing offers only by headline payout.

06

Traffic monetization

Design segmentation and routing logic that sends different users to the most relevant and economically sensible destination.

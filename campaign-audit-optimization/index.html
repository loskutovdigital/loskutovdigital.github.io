01 /

An audit should produce decisions, not a presentation full of observations.

The objective is to connect every significant finding to commercial impact, implementation effort and the evidence required to validate the change.

I examine campaign structure, source and placement performance, conversion events, costs, revenue, landing behaviour and the reliability of the data connecting them.

The output separates immediate fixes from experiments and longer-term structural changes, so the team knows what to do first and what should not be scaled yet.

02 /

Scope of work

01

Economics and KPI review

Evaluate ROI, ROAS, CPA, EPC, CVR and marginal efficiency at the most useful level of detail.

02

Traffic segmentation

Break performance down by source, placement, GEO, device, OS, audience and time.

03

Tracking integrity

Check whether missing, delayed or duplicated events distort the reported result.

04

Funnel analysis

Identify leakage across click, landing, registration, lead, sale and paid action.

05

Quality and fraud review

Look for anomalies, duplicates, mismatches and patterns that require validation.

06

Scaling readiness

Determine whether winning segments are robust enough for budget or coverage expansion.

Who this is for

For teams that need an independent answer before spending more.

The audit is suitable for advertisers, affiliate networks, media buying teams and businesses running paid acquisition where reports exist but confidence in the next decision is low.

Analysis is tied to execution.

Recommendations are written so an owner, buyer, tracking specialist or developer can understand the action, rationale, dependency and expected signal of improvement.

06 /

Frequently asked questions

What data do you need?

Campaign exports, cost and revenue data, tracking reports, funnel information and relevant partner terms are the usual starting point.

Can you audit a small account?

Yes. Scope is based on the decision and complexity, not only the spend level.

Will you make changes in the account?

Implementation can be included or delivered as a separate phase after the findings are agreed.

How is confidential data handled?

Access and data sharing should be limited to what is necessary, with confidentiality terms available where required.

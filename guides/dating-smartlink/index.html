Dating SmartLink: How Routing and Optimization Work

A dating SmartLink is a decision layer that receives a click, evaluates available signals and routes the user into an eligible dating funnel.

A SmartLink creates value only when it can make a better routing decision than a static link and prove that decision through data.

One link is convenient. The real question is what happens between the click and the destination.

This guide explains the operating model behind dating SmartLinks without assuming that every provider uses the same algorithm or exposes the same level of reporting.

The useful framework is consistent: eligibility first, routing second, conversion feedback third, and continuous measurement across the full identifier chain.

Eligibility must come before performance

A high-converting offer is irrelevant if it does not accept the user’s GEO, device, language, source or compliance context. Strong architecture separates eligibility from ranking.

First remove destinations that cannot legally or commercially accept the click. Only then compare the remaining options by rules or performance data.

Routing principle
Never allow historical EPC to override hard eligibility. A profitable but ineligible offer creates rejected traffic, compliance risk and misleading reports.
05 /

What “optimization” can mean

01

Rule-based routing

A fixed matrix sends known segments to predefined destinations.

02

Weighted rotation

Eligible offers receive traffic according to manually or automatically adjusted shares.

03

Performance routing

Recent conversion or revenue data influences destination selection.

04

Exploration logic

A portion of traffic tests alternatives instead of sending everything to the current winner.

05

Quality-aware routing

Approval or downstream value changes the score, not only initial conversion.

06

Fallback routing

Unmatched or capped traffic moves to a safe alternative rather than a dead end.

06 /

The black-box problem

Many SmartLinks do not expose the full offer pool, scoring method or route chosen for every click. That does not automatically make them ineffective, but it changes how the affiliate must test them.

When the destination logic is hidden, the affiliate needs stronger independent segmentation, source-level IDs, controlled comparison groups and payout reconciliation.

  • Preserve source, campaign and placement IDs before the SmartLink.
  • Compare performance by traffic segment, not only at total-link level.
  • Track approved revenue where possible, not only raw conversion count.
  • Maintain a direct-offer control when volume allows it.
  • Monitor route stability after payout, cap or inventory changes.
  • Document fallback behaviour for unsupported or low-volume traffic.

Minimum reporting standard

  • Clicks: total and unique, with clear timezone and filtering.
  • Conversions: event type, status and timestamp.
  • Revenue: payout value and currency, including later updates.
  • Dimensions: source, campaign, placement, GEO, device and subIDs.
  • Routing evidence: destination or offer identifier when the platform allows it.
  • Discrepancy workflow: logs or exports that can be compared with the affiliate tracker.

Frequently asked questions

Is a SmartLink the same as a tracker?

No. A tracker records and routes traffic according to configured campaigns. A SmartLink usually combines multiple eligible offers or funnels and selects a destination using network or platform logic.

Does a dating SmartLink guarantee the best offer?

No. It can only optimize using the inventory, signals, event data and model available to the provider.

Can I use a SmartLink with AdsBridge?

Yes. AdsBridge can act as your tracking and routing layer before or around a network SmartLink, provided identifiers and postbacks are mapped correctly.

Should I send all traffic to one SmartLink?

Not automatically. Proven segments may deserve direct offers, while mixed or uncovered traffic can remain in SmartLink routing.

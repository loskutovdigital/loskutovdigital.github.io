How to Choose a Dating CPA Network for Your Traffic

The right network is the one whose demand, validation model and operating process match the traffic you can actually deliver, not the network with the loudest landing page.

Start with your traffic profile, then evaluate the network. Reversing that order creates expensive tests with weak learning.

A useful network selection process converts broad promises into testable operational requirements.

Before contacting a network, write down your traffic source, placements, GEOs, device mix, audience, compliance constraints, daily volume and the event you can optimize toward.

Then score the network against those facts. This prevents a common failure mode: choosing an attractive offer first and discovering later that the traffic, tracking or validation model is incompatible.

05 /

Questions to ask the affiliate manager

  1. Which GEO, device and traffic-source combinations currently have real demand?
  2. What exact event is payable, and which validation reasons can remove it?
  3. Can I pass source, campaign and placement subIDs and receive them in the conversion postback?
  4. Can the postback include payout, currency, conversion status and event type?
  5. How often do caps, payouts and offer availability change, and how are changes communicated?
  6. What evidence is provided when traffic quality is challenged?
  7. Can I run a test conversion before launch?
  8. What is the hold period, payment threshold and reconciliation process?
  9. Does the SmartLink expose performance by GEO, device, source and subID?
  10. Who handles technical escalation when the account manager cannot resolve an issue?
06 /

Design a controlled network test

01Pre-qualificationShare an accurate traffic description and confirm written acceptance.
02Technical QATest redirects, identifiers, postbacks, payout and status mapping.
03Limited launchUse defined placements, spend limits and a short evaluation window.
04ReconciliationCompare source, tracker and network counts before judging performance.
05Quality reviewWait for approval or downstream feedback where the model requires it.
06Scale decisionIncrease volume only when approved economics and operations are stable.
07 /

What not to use as a selection criterion

01

Homepage superlatives

“Best”, “premium” and “AI-powered” do not explain the operational model.

02

Maximum advertised payout

The highest possible rate may not apply to your GEO, source or approval profile.

03

Offer count alone

A large catalogue is irrelevant if only a small part accepts your traffic.

04

Fast manager replies

Responsiveness matters, but technical accuracy and follow-through matter more.

05

Raw registration volume

Unapproved or low-quality leads can create a false sense of performance.

06

One-day EPC

Small samples and delayed validation can make early EPC misleading.

08 /

Decision output

At the end of the process, you should have a one-page decision record: accepted traffic, chosen model, links, payable event, tracking map, test budget, expected feedback window, payment terms and stop conditions.

Frequently asked questions

How many networks should I test at once?

Usually one or two controlled relationships are more useful than spreading a small budget across many networks. You need enough data to diagnose each test.

Can a network be good but still wrong for my traffic?

Yes. Network quality and traffic fit are separate questions. A reliable network may not have suitable demand for your source, GEO or audience.

Should I ask for a higher payout before testing?

First establish clean tracking, traffic quality and stable approved volume. Negotiation is stronger when supported by evidence.

Can you help review network terms privately?

Yes. A private review can evaluate terms, tracking, quality conditions and test design without publishing provider names.

Dating SmartLink vs Direct Offers

SmartLinks maximize coverage and reduce manual offer management. Direct offers maximize control. The right choice depends on traffic composition and the quality of your data.

Do not choose one model for the entire account. Choose the model for each traffic segment.

A mixed portfolio often outperforms an ideological “SmartLink only” or “direct only” approach.

SmartLinks and direct offers solve different operational problems. SmartLinks are useful when the affiliate cannot confidently select one destination before the click. Direct offers are useful when the audience, funnel and economics are already understood.

The practical strategy is to move segments between the two models as evidence improves.

Decision tree

Can you describe the user segment before the click?
Yes: test directYou have enough intent or targeting information to choose a specific funnel.
Is the segment too small for stable offer-level data?
Yes: use SmartLinkBroader pooled learning may be more useful than manual rotation.
Does one offer clearly outperform after approval?
Yes: move to directPreserve the winner and negotiate based on verified economics.
Do caps and availability change frequently?
Yes: keep fallbackA SmartLink or controlled fallback layer reduces dead traffic.
Must the creative match one exact destination?
Yes: prefer directCompliance and user expectation require destination control.
Is the traffic mixed across GEO, device or language?
Yes: segment firstUse direct for proven clusters and SmartLink for the remaining long tail.

Where direct offers usually win

01

High-intent search

Query and landing promise can match a specific user need.

02

Proven source-placement pair

Enough data exists to evaluate one funnel precisely.

03

Custom pre-lander

Run a fair comparison test

  1. Select one traffic segment with stable source, GEO, device and creative conditions.
  2. Keep the tracker, cost data and attribution window identical.
  3. Split traffic between the SmartLink and direct offer using a controlled percentage.
  4. Pass the same placement and campaign identifiers to both routes.
  5. Wait for the relevant approval or paid-event delay before judging the result.
  6. Compare approved EPC, variance, route stability and operational effort.
  7. Repeat only if the result is large enough to change a decision.
Avoid the false winner
A one-day result can favour the route with faster reporting rather than better economics. Match the evaluation window to the payable event.

The hybrid operating model

01ExploreUse SmartLink coverage to identify promising GEO, device and source combinations.
02IsolateMove repeatable segments into dedicated tracker campaigns.
03ValidateTest direct offers against the SmartLink control.
04PromoteAllocate more volume to the route with stronger approved economics.
05ProtectKeep a fallback path for caps, downtime and unsupported traffic.
06ReviewRe-test when payouts, inventory or traffic composition changes.
08 /

Measurement requirements

  • Independent click and cost tracking before the routing decision.
  • Stable source, campaign and placement IDs on both paths.
  • Conversion event, status and payout in the postback.
  • Approved rather than raw performance when quality validation exists.
  • A record of route changes, payout changes and offer downtime.
  • Enough sample size to separate real performance from random variance.

Frequently asked questions

Is a SmartLink always better for beginners?

It can simplify offer coverage, but beginners still need tracking and source-level data. Convenience does not remove the need for measurement.

Can I run a direct offer behind my own SmartLink?

Yes. Your routing layer can combine direct offers, network links and fallback destinations if eligibility and tracking are configured correctly.

What metric should decide the winner?

Use approved revenue per click or profit after traffic cost, measured over a window that captures the payable event.

How often should routes be re-tested?

Re-test when traffic composition, payout, offer availability, funnel or quality rules change materially.
